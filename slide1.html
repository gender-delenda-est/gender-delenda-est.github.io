What is K-Pop?

K-Pop emerged around 25 years ago in South Korea during a period of political and cultural liberalisation following the end of a sequence of repressive regimes and military dictatorships in 1987.

The end of censorship laws allowed the rise of new modes of media. Businesses in the South Korean cultural industries set themselves up to produce and cater for a new market for western-style pop music. In particular, over the mid-90s, the original 'big 3' of the K-Pop industry (SM, JYP, & YG) were all founded.

   

Hallyu

The rise of K-Pop as an internationally successful commodity and mode/site of cultural practice is closely linked to the idea of 'Hallyu'--the Korean wave. After the 1997 economic crisis, the South Korean government priortised cultural industries as an area of recovery. Along with K-Drama, K-Pop became a nationally-important export.
