K-Pop Fandom

Fandom is an absolutely integral part of strategies for marketing K-Pop (see Lee, 2016). A heuristic has emerged in recent scholarship (e.g. Jin, 2016; Lee & Nornes [Eds.], 2015) dividing Hallyu into "1.0" and "2.0" eras.

The transition between these is generally placed around 2008-2011, depending on the author.

Hallyu 1.0

More dominated by K-Drama than K-pop, and mostly confined to East Asia. This period saw less direct and concerted state intervention. Fans were generally older (30s-40s), and distribution mechanisms were generally older (e.g. terrestrial TV, radio).

Hallyu 2.0

Marked by a younger, more international fanbase (in particular expansion to Europe and the US), greater direct state intervention, and a move to newer digital methods of distribution.
