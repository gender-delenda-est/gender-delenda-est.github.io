Existing Research

Early Research and Culturalism

Much early research focusses almost wholly on questions of cultural analysis and on assessing the ostensible cultural sources of K-Pop and its specificity. For example:

  • Cho, 2005
  • Yang, 2007
  • Shin, 2009
  • Sun, 2011
  • Oh, 2013

Sociological Audience Studies

Ethnographic and sociological study of international K-Pop fans is actually surprisingly rare. A few studies have been carried out that provide some insights, especially into the early stages of the formation of K-Pop fandom within their respective subject countries. The most prominent and well research examples are:

  • Marinescu & Balica, 2013
  • Ko et al., 2014
  • Hubinette, 2012

Political-Economic and Critical Research

More recently, there has been an uptick in research interested in positioning K-Pop within a framework of more critical analysis, and in particular addressing the political-economic foundations for the phenomenon. In particular I recommend

  • Jin, 2016
  • Shin & Kim, 2013
  • Oh, 2018
  • Lee & Nornes [Eds.], 2015
